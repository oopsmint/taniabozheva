Inside the coaching room

Format & what to expect

Structure

Sessions happen weekly or bi-weekly and run for 60 minutes. We keep the day and time fixed, so a steady rhythm can build the depth this work needs.

In our first sessions we map your challenges and goals together. From there, each meeting connects what's happening in your life right now to the deeper patterns underneath it.

Between sessions: your work is to reflect on what we discussed. I also offer email support for short check-ins between meetings. Anything that needs depth, we bring into our next meeting.

My role & yours

I won't tell you what to do. That puts me in charge of your life, which isn't the point. My job is to help you think clearly, see your own patterns, and learn to trust your inner compass again.

Your part of the work:

  • Show up consistently
  • Reflect between sessions
  • Speak honestly (as much as possible)
  • Notice and share what shifts emotionally or in your life
On choosing a coach: the research is clear. The strongest predictor of progress isn't the modality, it's the quality of the relationship. That's why the first step is a free 20-minute call, so we can both feel whether the fit is right.

About me

I coach because I find people fascinating, and because I'm good at sitting with what's underneath.

Last year I left a stable corporate role to pursue coaching full time. I'd been doing this work for three and a half years and outgrew the rigid coaching format. I wanted to go deeper, so I went back to study psychology.

I currently work under the supervision of an experienced psychoanalytical therapist who was herself supervised by Otto Kernberg. That foundation is what lets me take the work past the symptom and into what's actually driving it.

"When a person can't find a deep sense of meaning, they distract themselves with pleasure."

Viktor Frankl

We reach for pleasure when we can't fill our reality with content that matters to us. That's often exactly where people find themselves when they come to me, and in my experience, it's the most interesting place to start from.
